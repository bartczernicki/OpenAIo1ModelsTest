Below is a comparison of Microsoft's risk factors from their 2023 and 2024 10-K filings, highlighting the changes between the two years.

| No. | Title | 2023 | 2024 | Change |
|-----|-------|------|------|--------|
| 1 | **Security of our information technology** | Discusses threats to IT security from various actors, including state-sponsored organizations, and methods used by attackers. Describes potential impacts of breaches. Mentions that the internal IT environment continues to evolve and may not keep pace with changes. | Similar to 2023, but adds disclosure of a specific cyber incident involving a nation-state actor gaining unauthorized access to Microsoft email accounts and source code repositories. Describes the potential impacts of this incident on reputation and customer relationships. | **Modified risk factor**: Addition of a specific cyber incident disclosure and its potential impacts. |
| 2 | **Security of our products, services, devices, and customersâ€™ data** | Emphasizes the importance of product and service security in customer decisions, challenges of security threats, and the impacts of vulnerabilities. Mentions that attacks can affect customers even if services are not directly compromised. | Similar content as 2023, but explicitly references the nation-state attack described in the prior risk factor as having affected customers. Highlights that attacks on Microsoft's IT infrastructure have affected customers and may do so in the future. | **Modified risk factor**: References the specific cyber incident affecting customers. |
| 3 | **Development and deployment of defensive measures** | Highlights the need to continuously enhance security measures and the potential costs associated. Failure to do so can harm reputation and lead customers to seek alternatives. | Similar content as 2023. Emphasizes timely deployment of software updates and meeting specialized customer expectations. | **Minor modifications**: Emphasis on timely updates and specialized customer needs. |
| 4 | **Disclosure and misuse of personal data could result in liability and harm our reputation.** | Discusses risks related to the disclosure or misuse of customer or user data, and the potential impacts on reputation and legal exposure. Notes the challenges in protecting against data breaches. | Similar to 2023, but adds that despite efforts to continuously improve security controls, Microsoft may fail to identify or mitigate insider threat activities that could lead to misuse of systems or data. | **Modified risk factor**: Includes risks related to insider threats and misuse of systems. |
| 5 | **We may not be able to protect information in our products and services from use by others.** | Covers the risk that changes in law or interpretations may weaken Microsoft's ability to prevent third parties from scraping or gathering information, affecting product value. | Similar content as 2023 but states that this could adversely affect business, financial condition, and results of operations. | **Slight modification**: Includes potential adverse effects on financial condition and results. |
| 6 | **Abuse of our platforms may harm our reputation or user engagement.** Advertising, professional, marketplace, and gaming platform abuses_ | Notes that reputation or user engagement may be negatively affected by hostile or inappropriate activity, which may require substantial investment to prevent or respond to. | Similar content, but mentions that such activities could adversely affect business, financial condition, and results of operations. Emphasizes potential unsuccessful investments in people and technology to combat abuses. | **Slight modification**: Includes potential adverse effects on financial condition and results. |
| 7 | **Abuse of our platforms may harm our reputation or user engagement.** Other digital safety abuses_ | Mentions that hosted services may be used to disseminate harmful or illegal content, which may negatively affect reputation and may result in regulatory oversight or liability if content is not properly moderated. | Similar content, but states that failure to comply with content moderation regulations may subject Microsoft to enhanced regulatory oversight, civil or criminal liability, or reputational damage, adversely affecting business, financial condition, and results of operations. | **Slight modification**: Highlights increased regulatory risks and potential adverse effects on financial condition and results. |
| 8 | **The development of the IoT presents security, privacy, and execution risks.** | Addresses risks associated with IoT, including potential defects, security vulnerabilities, and compliance with data handling requirements, which may lead to legal claims or enforcement actions. | This risk factor has been broadened and modified. In 2024, it is titled: "**Our products and services, how they are used by customers, and how third-party products and services interact with them, may present security, privacy, and execution risks.**" It includes similar concerns but in a broader context, including customers' use and third-party interactions, and mentions AI products and high-risk scenarios. | **Modified risk factor**: Broadened scope from IoT to include products and services usage by customers and third parties, introduces risks related to AI products, customer use, and third-party interactions. |
| 9 | **Issues in the development and use of AI may result in reputational or competitive harm or liability.** | Discusses risks and challenges presented by AI, including flawed algorithms, biased datasets, and potential for AI-generated content to be offensive or harmful, which could result in legal claims or brand harm. Mentions ethical issues and impacts on society. | Similar content, but adds that risks may arise from current copyright infringement and other claims related to AI training and output, new and proposed legislation and regulations, such as the European Union's AI Act and the U.S.'s AI Executive Order, and new applications of data protection, privacy, consumer protection, intellectual property, and other laws. | **Modified risk factor**: Expanded to include specific regulatory developments, legal risks related to AI, and mentions of new legislation and regulations. |
